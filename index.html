People Also Ask (FAQs)

It's generally recommended to visit a dental clinic at least twice a year—every six months—for a professional teeth cleaning and dental exam to help prevent gum disease, cavities, and to catch issues early.

A standard session with a dental hygienist includes scaling and planing to remove plaque and tartar (even below the gumline), polishing, flossing, and a fluoride treatment to protect enamel and prevent future problems.

A routine dental cleaning (or prophylaxis) is for patients with healthy gums and focuses on removing plaque and tartar above the gumline. In contrast, a deep cleaning targets buildup below the gumline and is used to treat early signs of gum (periodontal) disease.
